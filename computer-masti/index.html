Computer Science in Schools

CMC: A Model Computer Science Curriculum for K-12 Schools, Technical Report, (TR-CSE-2013-52), Dept. of Computer Science and Engg, IIT Bombay.
This document defines the philosophy and pedagogy underlying the creation of the Computer Masti series of books.

    • Brief History
    2016 : Next Education acquires Computer Masti business from InOpen. IPR transferred from InOpen to Next Education. Next Education continues to make the above books available for free download.
    2009 : Inopen Technologies incubated at SINE-IITB, for large-scale implementation. IPR transferred from IIT Bombay to InOpen. InOpen decides to keep the above books available for free download. [InOpen co-founders: Sridhar Iyer and Rupesh Shah]
    2008 - 2010 : Pilot implementation at SSRVM Mulund, and teacher training at IIT Bombay, by Authors team below.
    Here are a Summary Report, the Full-Report in Plain Text version, and the PDF version with photos (12 MB).
    2007 - 2013 : Creation of Computer Masti textbooks, and release of textbooks in Creative Commons. IPR with IIT Bombay. [Team - Editor: Sridhar Iyer. Content Authors: Vijayalakshmi Chitta, Farida Khan, Usha Vishwanathan, Malati Baru. Illustrator: Kaumudi Sahasrabudhe. Design and DTP: Sameer Sahasrabudhe, Swati Revandkar, Sandeep Gaikwad.]
    2006 - 2007 : Writing of the first version of the CMC curriculum. [Team - Sridhar Iyer and Malati Baru.]

    2 Install Ubuntu Here are some guidelines to Install Ubuntu on your computer after you have downloaded it: See this Video.
    3 Project OSCAR Project OSCAR (Open Source Courseware Animations Repository) is a repository of interactive animations for teaching various concepts. The repository currently hosts several School level animations, and UG/PG level Learning objects. The current goal of Project OSCAR is to develop animations for Undergraduate and Post-Graduate Levels.
